01

Color

Indigo and yellow, carried from a logo and resume I made in 2010. Indigo is the brand; yellow is the highlighter — it marks what matters, never wallpaper.

Surfaces: Paper (#F6F3EE) is the page. White (#FFFFFF) is reserved for raised, grouped containers — a card, panel, or frame around related content. Indigo (#27075A) is the inversion moment, used once. White is never a general background; it always means "this is a contained module."

05

How the system is built

A real sequence. Product design feeds the pattern layer, which feeds the visual layer, which feeds implementation.

STEP 01

Product design

Real product work surfaces the pattern — what needs flexibility before anything is codified.

STEP 02

Pattern & guidelines

Patterns codified with the rules and the edge cases caught early.

STEP 03

Visual layer

Tokens and components in Figma — the durable, reusable surface.

STEP 04

Implementation

Shipped and governed in code, tracked so it stays aligned over time.

The thinking is the system.
